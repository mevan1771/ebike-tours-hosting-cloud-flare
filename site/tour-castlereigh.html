Castlereigh Reservoir: Tea Trails & Water Loop

Hatton start · shoreline roads · bridges & tea estates

5–6 hrs Moderate ~30–40 km Start: Hatton
Book now See route

Why you'll love it

Escape into the emerald heart of Sri Lanka’s Tea Country on this stunning cycling loop around the Castlereigh Reservoir. Starting from the historic hub of Hatton, you'll pedal along quiet, winding roads that hug the shoreline, offering continuous panoramic views of the water and the undulating tea-covered hills. Cross iconic suspension bridges, witness colonial-era estate life, and breathe the freshest mountain air. This route offers a perfect balance of moderate challenge and unforgettable natural beauty, capturing the serene essence of the Central Highlands.

Step 1

Meet in Hatton Town & Route Briefing

Begin your adventure in the bustling railway town of Hatton. After a warm welcome, receive your fitted mountain bike and a detailed safety briefing. We'll outline the day's loop and provide insight into the rich tea history of the area you are about to explore.
Step 2

Ascending through Dickoya & Norwood Tea Estates

Your ride begins with a gentle ascent, winding through the historic Dickoya and Norwood estate regions. Cycle past iconic colonial bungalows and active tea factories. This is the perfect introduction to the sights and sounds of the working Tea Country.
Step 3

The Iconic Castlereigh Dam and Reservoir Viewpoint

Reach the highlight of the tour! We pause at a viewpoint offering a magnificent vista of the Castlereigh Reservoir Dam and the sparkling waters beyond. Capture incredible photos of the vast blue expanse contrasting with the surrounding green carpet of tea.
Step 4

Tranquil Cycling along the Reservoir Banks

The core of the loop features peaceful cycling along the reservoir's edge. This gentle stretch offers continuous water views and leads you across the stunning, low-level Castlereigh Bridge, which often appears to float just above the water line.
Step 5

Discovering Hidden Tea Trails & Estate Life

We diverge onto smaller estate roads, spotting secluded natural pools and seasonal cascades. Interact with local Tamil tea community members and witness the meticulous process of tea plucking and gathering, gaining insight into their daily lives.
Step 6

Return Loop to Hatton & Post-Ride Refreshments

The final stage is a gentle downhill pedal back towards Hatton, completing the loop. Celebrate your achievement with traditional Sri Lankan tea and snacks before we arrange your final transfer/wrap-up.
